Common Mistakes When Using MotionOne Library (And How to Avoid Them)

1. Ignoring the AI Suggestions

Mistake: Some users overlook the AI-powered recommendations, leading to less polished animations.

Solution:

  • Always check the AI suggestions before finalizing.
  • Experiment with different styles to see what works best for your audience.

2. Not Customizing Templates Enough

Mistake: Simply slapping your brand logo on a template without adjusting motion or timing can make animations look generic.

Solution:

  • Replace placeholder elements with your own assets.
  • Adjust animation speed to match your brand’s tone (e.g., fast-paced for tech, slow for luxury).

3. Skipping the Style Transfer Step

Mistake: Forgetting to apply brand consistency can lead to incohesive visuals across campaigns.

Solution:

  • Always use the "Apply Brand Style" feature before exporting.
  • Test variations to ensure the style feels natural.

4. Overcomplicating Animations

Mistake: Adding too many effects can make animations distracting or confusing.

Solution:

  • Start simple—focus on one key motion per scene.
  • Use the AI to suggest minimalist animations that still convey impact.

5. Not Leveraging Batch Processing for Bulk Work

Mistake: Manually editing multiple files wastes time and increases errors.

Solution:

  • Use batch processing for updates, color changes, or template swaps.
  • Automate repetitive tasks to free up time for creativity.

6. Forgetting to Optimize for Platforms

Mistake: Exporting animations without checking platform-specific settings can lead to poor performance.

Solution:

  • Always use the "Export for Social Media" tool.
  • Adjust frame rates and resolutions based on the platform.

7. Not Using Voice-to-Motion for Scripts

Mistake: Writing scripts manually can be time-consuming and inconsistent.

Solution:

  • Use the AI script generator to create engaging narratives.
  • Test voice recordings to ensure animations match the tone.

8. Ignoring Analytics for Future Improvements

Mistake: Not tracking performance data means missing opportunities to refine future animations.

Solution:

  • Link social media accounts to MotionOne’s dashboard.
  • Use AI insights to adjust pacing, style, and content for better engagement.
